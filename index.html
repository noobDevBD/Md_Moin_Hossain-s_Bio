The proposed network will consist of multiple lines and serve a large part of the city, including the city center, airports, and universities. Whould you like to read some fun facts about Bangladesh?

Bangladesh is a South Asian country with a rich history, vibrant culture, and unique characteristics. Here are some interesting facts about Bangladesh:


Royal Bengal Tiger

The Sundarbans, the largest mangrove forest in the world, is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger. It is one of the few places where these majestic tigers can be found.


Largest River Delta

The country is known for having the world's largest river delta, formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers. This delta is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.


Bangla Language

Bangladesh is the only country in the world where the official language, Bengali (Bangla), is spoken by the majority of the population. The International Mother Language Day, observed on February 21, commemorates the Bengali Language Movement in 1952.


Cox's Bazar

This coastal town is home to one of the longest sandy beaches in the world, stretching over 120 kilometers along the Bay of Bengal.


Liberation War

Bangladesh gained independence from Pakistan in 1971 after a nine-month-long war of independence. The country celebrates its Victory Day on December 16 to mark this historic event.


Microcredit Pioneer

Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and social entrepreneur, was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for founding Grameen Bank and pioneering the concepts of microcredit and microfinance.


Jute Capital

Bangladesh is one of the world's largest producers and exporters of jute, a natural fiber used in making textiles, carpets, and packaging materials.


Rickshaw Art

Dhaka, the capital city, is known for its vibrant and colorful rickshaws. The rickshaw art often features intricate paintings, bright colors, and elaborate designs, making them unique to the region.


Rocket Steamer

The historic Rocket Steamer is a paddle-wheel steamer that has been operating on Bangladesh's rivers since the early 20th century, providing a nostalgic and scenic journey.


Mango Capital

Bangladesh is famous for its delicious mangoes. The country is one of the largest producers of mangoes in the world, and the fruit is a significant part of the local culture and cuisine.


These facts showcase the diverse and culturally rich aspects of Bangladesh.
